The ABB bar

The dark grey ABB bar provides consistency and familiarity across ABB’s software interfaces, and typically contains logo, product name, main navigation, global functions and critical information. In addition to being an important brand element, it helps the user’s perception of system structure.

Main characteristics

  1. Overview of system structure and/or system status.
  2. A simple, slim, dark grey bar with limited use of color.
  3. Fixed position. A top bar by default, but it can also be presented as a vertical side bar or bottom bar in order to support specific product needs.

Content

Identity

The default version contains a white ABB logo and a discrete white product name.

There are a few exceptions:

  • Small screens (e.g. Mobile)
  • ABB logo on the display hardware
  • Specific customer deliveries might require the ABB logo to be hidden or replaced by a customer logo. In cases like this the dark grey bar still provides an important ABB feel.

Main navigation

If the product contains different sections or views, the ABB bar will give you an overview and easy access to these through navigation controls such as:

  • Tabs or tab groups
  • Dropdown menus
  • Mega menus

Note that in order to keep the navigation across all applications consistent, the bar should only be used for global top level navigation and main functions. Page-specific tools and sub-navigation should be placed within the main view.

Status and notifications

Selected top level system status information and alerts can be presented on the bar. Examples are badges for user tasks and notifocations and alarm/fault information.

Global tools

The bar may also contain support tools, including:

  • User information
  • User account menu
  • System menu
  • Help
  • Search
  • Language support

Dos and don’ts

Logo and product name

Do not use ABB logo + Claim.

Do not use the red ABB logo.

Do not combine the ABB logo with a product logo / wordmark. The product name should be written in the font used elsewhere in the application.

(Update illustration) Logo and product name may be hidden, either because of limited space (mobile), or due to product / customer requirements or usage context.

Logo placement

Do not place the logo in the middle part of the bar. It shall always be positioned in a corner, either to the left or right side of the interface.

It is allowed to place the logo + product name to the right of a menu button if separated clearly.

Use equal margins between grouped elements to the extent feasible to create order, rythm and structure.

Protective field

The logo must always be surrounded by a defined minimum space of 0.5 vh (vh = vertical height), separating it from other design elements.

Do not use a blurry or pixelated logo

Colors

Do not use bright colors on large areas. Colors should be limited to highlighted items, status indications and notifications.

Placement

The bar can be either placed on top (primary), on the sides or in the bottom (secondary) of the interface. The choice of where to place the bar for your application should be based on platform/technology conventions along with usability considerations.

If the ABB logo is used, it shall always placed in one of the four corners and always on the ABB bar.
