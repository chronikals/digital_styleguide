Color philosophy

Our products are used in a wide variety of contexts and thus meet equally diverse color requirements: high contrast for outdoor use, low contrast for high performance HMIs, commercial appeal, industry standards, specific customer requirements, etc.

In some products, such as control room interfaces, the main goal is to enable users to detect and mitigate operational disturbances. In others, such as engineering tools or ERP tools, the core challenge is to help the users be efficient, correct and complete when entering data into complex tables. To cover these contradictory needs our color palette is based on two basic principles:

Color conveys meaning

Use of color should have a purpose. In general, colors should be used sparingly in order to draw attention to important elements, or as visual support in situations that require the userâ€™s attention.

Information layering

Choose a color palette that creates distinct visual layers with different levels of salience/intensity. The layers should match the importance of the information:

  1. Abnormal/critical states, or states that require attention: alarms, errors, notifications
  2. Normal dynamic information: text, values, trend graphs
  3. Static elements: contextual information, compartments, labels
  4. Backgrounds

Always test colors on target output devices. You may have to make adjustments tothe actual color values in order to get the right appearance, contrast and clarity.
