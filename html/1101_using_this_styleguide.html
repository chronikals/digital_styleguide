Using this style guide

This model illustrates how the style guide and Common UX team mainly act as a resource in the later steps of a design process, when visual design is applied. All conceptual design decisions must be made within the product team. This includes content, functionality and interaction models, layout and navigation.

It is highly recommended that product teams include professional designers,either internal or contracted, to ensure a user-centered design process.

Criteria for using this style guide

There exists several style guides within ABB already, and it might not always be entirely clear when to follow which style guide. Use these criteria to decide whether this style guide is appropriate for your case.

Use this style guide if one or more of the following applies:

your product is sold to customers or your product is bundled together with products (HW or SW) that are sold to customers

your product is used to deliver services to customers, including engineering of systems and products

your product is typically used by customers in their daily work

your product presents data from your customer’s plant or equipment

The style guide is not primarily intended for:

sales or marketing tools

tools to communicate with customers

tools that present information about ABB product or service offerings

internal or external web sites
