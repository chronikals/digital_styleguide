About this style guide

This document describes the ABB look & feel for software products. Many of our customers and end-users see ABB primarily through the day-to-day interactions with the software products we deliver. Providing professional, modern and harmonized user interfaces is a basic requirement in today’s market.

This style guide is valid for all types of software from all divisions and business units within ABB. The primary target applications are those that are either sold to customers or delivered to customers as part of service agreements. Other applications, such as engineering tools or internal tools are also free to follow this style guide.

This style guide has been produced by the Common UX project, a part of ABB’s cross-division SW2020 initiative. For more information, please visit

https://sites.abb.com/sites/GlobalSoftwareStrategyInitiatives/CommonUX/

Using this style guide

This model illustrates how the style guide and Common UX team mainly act as a resource in the later steps of a design process, when visual design is applied. All conceptual design decisions must be made within the product team. This includes content, functionality and interaction models, layout and navigation.

It is highly recommended that product teams include professional designers,either internal or contracted, to ensure a user-centered design process.

Criteria for using this style guide

There exists several style guides within ABB already, and it might not always be entirely clear when to follow which style guide. Use these criteria to decide whether this style guide is appropriate for your case.

Use this style guide if one or more of the following applies:

your product is sold to customers or your product is bundled together with products (HW or SW) that are sold to customers

your product is used to deliver services to customers, including engineering of systems and products

your product is typically used by customers in their daily work

your product presents data from your customer’s plant or equipment

The style guide is not primarily intended for:

sales or marketing tools

tools to communicate with customers

tools that present information about ABB product or service offerings

internal or external web sites

Hierarchy of style guides

ABB UI style guide

This style guide is intended to be an overarching, top-level guide valid for all SW products in all divisions in ABB. It is intended to provide the basic level of harmonization and familiarity between ABB products.

Product or BU-level style guides (optional)

Some BUs and products might choose to develop a more detailed style guide to create a closer alignment with less variability between products or screens, within the boundaries set by the ABB UI style guide. Typically this can specify further a limited set of layout options, narrow down the choice of colors used and specify interaction patterns.

Product specifications

Typically, the handover from interaction designers to the product developers will still be the detailed, screen by screen specifications necessary to develop the product. By applying the style guides above the specification process will be more efficient as much will already be defined above, and similar from product to product.

How to get started

1Contact the UI/UX design responsible within your organization. If there is nobody within your organization or BU, contact the CommonUX team directly to get startup guidance.

2Decide on appropriate process for your case together with designer. The process will vary according to:

  • ambition level
  • development process
  • scope of development project (new product, new feature or redesign of existing product)

3Get designs reviewed by CommonUX team before major implementation direction is set, and your application reviewed after implementation.
